The World Wildlife Fund (WWF) is a leading conservation organization operating in over 100 countries. For more than 50 years, WWF has worked to preserve the natural world, help communities manage natural resources, and promote sustainable policies and markets. Visit their website here.

Bioversity International was founded in 1974 as the International Board for Plant Genetic Resources (IBPGR) and later renamed in 2006 to reflect its expanded role. It focuses on agricultural and forest biodiversity and supports development through genetic resource conservation. Visit their website here.

Fauna & Flora International, based in the UK, is a conservation charity with over 120 global projects. Originally formed to preserve wild fauna, it now works in nearly 50 countries to protect species and habitats worldwide. Visit their website here.
